OBJECTIVE

Build a virtual agent that functions as a health coach. The scope of this project focused on guiding patients with type 2 diabetes in improving eating habits.

This project was part of a larger proposal by The OSU College of Medicine to determine whether virtual health coaches (VHCs) can improve glycemic control and other outcomes in patients with type 2 diabetes.


During the summer of 2014, I was funded by the Bennett Medical Student Research Scholarship to develop a prototype.

BACKGROUND

Understanding health coaching and type 2 diabetes patients

To better understand the project vision, users (patients with type 2 diabetes), and coaching techniques, I interviewed stakeholders and conducted secondary research including an ecosystem dump, literature review, and internet research to answer the following questions:

  • What makes health coaching effective?
  • What are essential health coaching techniques?
  • What are type 2 diabetes patients' greatest concerns with regards to eating habits?
  • What are their goals?

What is a health coach?

A health coach is a professional who guides patients in setting behavioral health goals, creating an action plan to achieve those goals, and recognizing and working through challenges.


  • Shown to be effective in improving health measures such as BMI and blood sugar in patients with diabetes
  • Does not give medical advice
  • Provides resources and guidance
  • Asks self-reflective questions
  • Helps patients learn to independently manage their own health

Patients fear long-term consequences

I learned about patient perspectives through diabetes support forums as well as patient interview videos and created personas to summarize and share these findings.

Improving eating habits to lower health risks

For patients with type 2 diabetes, having a healthy diet is a vital part of controlling blood sugar and preventing long-term consequences.


Health coaches help these patients set small achievable goals to work towards healthier habits overall.

Potential benefit of virtual agents

Studies on health issues such as PTSD have shown that virtual health coaches can potentially provide greater benefit to patients because there is less concern of being judged.


Also, virtual health coaches are more accessible than professionals because they can be accessed anywhere.

ITERATION 1

Conversational user interface (CUI) design and agent persona

The initial goal was to create a functioning prototype of a CUI that can ask patients relevant health coaching questions and respond appropriately.


Key conversation pathways

I first mapped out the key conversation topics and pathways that could occur within the scope of improving eating habits. This also included miscellaneous topics such as introductions, closings, chit chat, and errors (not understanding the user or having a specific response).

Agent persona

We decided to use an animated 3D model for the agent given the existing work done at OSU and the ability to simulate a more realistic experience of interacting with a coach. We discussed ideas for the future such as including VHCs of different genders, ages, ethnicities, and art styles.


We focused on the following characteristics:

  • Friendly, calm, and professional
  • Cartoon-like appearance to reduce the uncanny valley effect
  • Relaxing but professional environment

Conversation flow diagrams

To map out the actual conversations and script, I created flow diagrams for different scenarios, accounting for different potential user responses.

CREATING THE PROTOTYPE

Managing a conversation with infinite possibilities

I used my knowledge about health coaching techniques and programming in ChatScript (the AI engine) to develop the conversational functionality of the VHC.


Team member Kellen Maicher, MFA, modeled and animated the 3D character and environment.

Development, testing, & iteration

The character was represented visually by an animated 3D model, and the two systems were integrated in game engine Unity. Finally, the application was combined with a survey and instructions on a web interface.


We sent the prototype to stakeholders to test. As expected, a lot of programming issues and awkward conversations were discovered and subsequently corrected.

Report and presentation

The research, planning process, prototype development, and evaluation were compiled into a final report and poster presentation. I presented this poster at OSU Research Day on April 15, 2015.

CONCLUSION AND NEXT STEPS

Potential to empower people struggling to achieve health goals


Overall this was a project I was very passionate about since I am interested in digital health. I believe that a virtual health coach could potentially be very useful for patients who do not have access to an actual professional health coach or just want a bit of guidance without worrying about being judged by another human.


Health coaching itself is proven to be an effective technique in improving health outcomes. With the guidance of professional health coaches, the VHC could become a very effective health application.

Next Steps

Though I moved on to another job after this project, if I were to continue it the next steps of this project would involve:

  • Conducting user research with actual Type 2 Diabetes patients, since my research was conducted using diabetes forums and interviews
  • Building a more robust prototype with the newly designed interface
  • Reviewing and testing with stakeholders
  • Making improvements based on the testing and feedback and testing the VHC with actual patient volunteers.
