OBJECTIVE

Facilitate better communication and collaboration between customers, electrical experts, and the switchgear they are working with during troubleshooting.


A huge part of the company's customer experience is that of troubleshooting switchgear when power goes out. Electricians, facility managers, field service engineers, and more face these challenges daily. Through improving communication and collaboration, our goal is to enhance safety, reliability, efficiency, and the human experience of power management system.

UNDERSTANDING

Deep dive into power management

The first few months we spent focusing on primary and secondary research into the domain of power management to understand the different people involved, their needs and tasks, and the switchgear they work with.

What are switchgear?

Switchgear are like fuse boxes, but for large buildings and complexes such as hospitals and universities. They are devices used to protect and control electrical equipment.


Switchgear can contain many components including circuit breakers, fuses, and protective relays. Within a certain distance from switchgear, there can be risk of an arc flash, a type of electrical explosion.

Affinity diagramming

From our 17 interviews, we organized over 700 affinity notes to discover themes and patterns. We then conducted a walk-the-wall exercise with our client to share and discuss findings and inspire new ideas and visions.

Diagnosing and troubleshooting problems

A huge part of our client's customer experience is that of troubleshooting switchgear when power goes out. Through our research, we discovered the many challenges, costs, and risks that exist with this process.

Diagram of pain points in the process of troubleshooting switchgear

VISIONING

Emerging technologies can provide the power to make interactions with switchgear smoother and safer.

To tackle the various pain points we discovered through our research, we brainstormed many different ideas, many of which utilized emerging technologies such as augmented reality and conversational design to provide information more efficiently and in context.

Sketching concepts

From 29 different sketches and ideas, we eventually narrowed down to 11 concepts to evaluate with users, advisors, and peers.

Concept evaluations

These 11 concepts were presented as storyboards to different users, including customers, field service engineers, and remote support teams. Users provided their reactions, what they could see being useful or not, and eventually we narrowed down to 4 concepts to further refine.

Prototyping

I focused on prototyping the interactive 3D manual concept, including creating low to mid-fidelity designs, creating an interactive prototype with InVision, and collaborating with teammates on implementation.

User testing

Interactive prototypes were built for the 4 selected concepts. We then conducted user testsing to elicit reactions from users and further refine the final vision.

FINAL CONCEPT

Lumière, a contextual support tool for switchgear

We envision a system called Lumière that facilitates efficient diagnosis and troubleshooting of switchgear among electricians and engineers via an integrated set of knowledge sharing and decision-making tools for quick access to key contextual information about switchgear.
